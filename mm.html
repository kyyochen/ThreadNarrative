Maison Margiela

Maison Margiela, founded by Belgian designer Martin Margiela in 1988, is a French luxury fashion house renowned for its unconventional designs that deconstruct traditional garment structures and challenge the norms of the fashion industry. The brand is known for its anonymity and collective approach to design, with Margiela himself famously shunning the spotlight. Maison Margiela's aesthetic is characterized by raw, unfinished details, oversized proportions, and the use of recycled materials, reflecting a deep commitment to conceptual fashion and craftsmanship. The iconic Tabi boot, with its split-toe design, is among the brand's most recognizable pieces. Maison Margiela has also been influential in popularizing the concept of "replica" garments, reproducing and reinterpreting vintage clothing and objects. Under the creative direction of John Galliano since 2014, the brand has infused its avant-garde roots with a new level of opulence and drama, while continuing to push the boundaries of traditional fashion.
